Metal roofing

Metal roofing that lasts decades

Metal roofs stand up to hail, heat, and wind. Standing seam systems are sleek and thermally efficient; exposed-fastener options work for smaller commercial spaces.

How we handle Metal roofing

We measure, design the seam layout, order materials, and install with precision flashing and underlayment for Gulf Coast durability.

  • Design & material selection — Discuss standing seam vs. exposed-fastener, color, finish, and budget. We measure the roof, model the seam layout, and show you samples.
  • Preparation & material delivery — Order materials from trusted suppliers. Prepare the deck, install underlayment and trim components, and stage coil on site.
  • Installation & pressure testing — Roll-form seams on-site or install pre-formed panels, flash all penetrations (vents, skylights, chimneys), and inspect for watertight integrity before final walkthrough.

Signs you may need this service

Consider metal roofing if you're looking to upgrade lifespan, energy efficiency, or weather resistance.

  • Your current asphalt roof is 15+ years old and approaching end of life
  • You want a hail-proof, wind-resistant upgrade (140+ mph rating available)
  • High cooling bills—metal's solar reflectance cuts AC load in Houston heat
  • You're concerned about moisture or condensation in attic spaces with humidity
  • You want a single roof that will outlast your mortgage

FAQ — Metal roofing

How long does a metal roof last?

Metal roofing typically lasts 40–70 years depending on finish and maintenance. It resists hail, wind to 140+ mph, and reflects solar heat—lowering cooling costs in Houston's climate.

What's the difference between standing seam and exposed-fastener metal?

Standing seam is hidden-fastener (no visible screws; thermal movement is accommodated by the seam). Exposed-fastener uses visible screws, is lower cost, and suits metal carports or agricultural buildings. Standing seam is premium for homes.

Can metal roofs leak?

Yes, but underlayment and flashing are critical. We use high-temperature ice-and-water shield, stainless fasteners, and proper drainage details so water cannot pool or wick under the panels.
