Flat / low-slope roofing

Flat & low-slope roofing with proper drainage

Flat roofs need engineered drainage or they'll pool and leak. TPO and modified systems, when designed correctly, are durable and energy-efficient for Houston commercial and multi-family buildings.

How we handle Flat / low-slope roofing

We assess deck condition, design drainage routing, install underlayment and membrane, and test for leaks before handoff.

  • Deck assessment & drainage design — Inspect deck structure, measure square footage, assess existing drainage, and identify interior drains, gutters, or scuppers.
  • Removal & installation — Remove old membrane, repair soft spots, install new underlayment and sloped crickets if needed, then roll membrane and flash all penetrations.
  • Testing & verification — Test flashing and seams with water hose; inspect from interior for leaks; clean gutters and verify drainage flow before final walkthrough.

Signs you may need this service

Your flat roof may need replacement if you notice these issues.

  • Visible pooling or slow drainage after heavy rain or Gulf storms
  • Interior water stains, soft deck, or mold growth in attic or top floor
  • Seams cracking, membrane blistering, or surface deterioration (chalking)
  • Gutters or drains clogged with debris; downspouts undersized for storm runoff
  • Roof is 15+ years old and showing wear or multiple past patches

FAQ — Flat / low-slope roofing

Can you install a truly flat roof?

Flat roofs require pitched gutters, interior drains, or scuppers to shed water—no true "flat" roof is perfectly level. We design a minimum 1:40 slope (1" per 40 ft run) so water sheds quickly in Houston's frequent rains.

What's the difference between TPO and modified bitumen?

TPO (thermoplastic polyolefin) is single-ply, white, energy-efficient, and lasts 15–25 years. Modified bitumen is two-ply asphalt with rubberized coating, lasts 15–20 years, and costs less. We recommend TPO for most Houston applications due to heat reflectance.

How do you prevent pooling on flat roofs?

Pools form when gutters clog, drains fail, or the deck settles. We install properly sized gutters, clean-out caps, and secondary drain protection to prevent standing water.
